Rule 5 — Pyramid Entry (OD) 📊 View Chart ↗
Opening Drive breakdown = add lots as each candle confirms rejection.
When price breaks down below a triple confluence (Demand Zone + PDL + S1) on the opening candle, this is an Opening Drive (OD) — not a random spike. Each subsequent candle that tries but fails to reclaim S1 is fresh confirmation. Add lots progressively as the market proves the breakdown is real.
🔑 OD Pattern vs Extreme Candle Reversal — How to Tell
✅ OD Pattern (Add Lots)
Price stays below S1 after breakdown — pullbacks attempt to reclaim but candles close below the level. Each rejection is a new entry signal.
❌ Extreme Candle Reversal (Step Aside)
Price closes back above S1 quickly after the initial breakdown — sellers are trapped, buyers are in control. Do not add; exit existing position.
1
Opening candle closes below Demand Zone + PDL + S1 (triple confluence) → Enter Lot 1
2
Second candle attempts to reclaim S1 but cannot close above it → Add Lot 2 on that failure
3
Third candle is a red rejection candle from S1 → Add Lot 3 — trend fully confirmed
SL ABOVE S1 Single SL for all lots — above the broken S1 level
Target CPR or 2R CPR lower band or 2R from Lot 1 entry — whichever comes first
Key principle: You are not guessing direction — you are letting the market prove it candle by candle. Lot 1 is the thesis. Lot 2 is the confirmation. Lot 3 is the conviction. If any candle closes back above S1, the OD is invalidated — treat it as an Extreme Candle Reversal and exit all lots.

Reference (27 Mar 26, Nifty 5min): Opening candle broke below Demand Zone + PDL + S1. Candles 2–3 failed to close above S1. Candle 3 was a red rejection. Price trended down ~200pts to 22,835 by EOD.

Rule 6
Wide CPR — First Touch of P is the Best Spread Entry

On Wide CPR days with a sideways bullish bias, the first clean 5-min candle close at or just above the Central Pivot (P) is the highest-conviction entry for a Debit Spread.

  • First test of P: Take at full size — fresh support discovery, maximum edge.
  • Re-test of P (60+ min later): Valid but lower quality — take at half size. Market spent time hovering at P, conviction is reduced.
  • Do NOT require foreknowledge of how many times P will be tested. Entry logic is purely: "Clean candle close at/above P → enter spread."

BUY strike = P level (support) | SELL strike = nearest resistance above | Min spread width ₹100

Observed: Nifty 5-min chart, 6 Apr 2026 — Two entries at P (10:00 AM and 11:15 AM). First touch was the superior entry.
